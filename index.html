Animals in Cinema

Whether they star as a furriendly fuzzball or crrrageous companion, cats and dogs have been featured in movies as early as the 19th century! Through their close relationship with humans, cats and dogs have made an appearance in thousands of movies and TV shows, acting in roles as funny, intelligent, and cute creatures!

But their influence doesn’t stop there. From viral internet memes and brand mascots to pet adoption campaigns and booming pet product industries, cats and dogs continue to shape the way we express affection, humor, and companionship in modern life. Our data visualizations explore how these beloved animals compare across different realms — from media popularity and adoption rates to the growing market that revolves around them.

Ownership and Shelter Statistics

Does media play a role in how Americans choose which pet to own? Let's take a look!

2024 AVMA Pet Ownership Statistics

It is undeniable that dogs are the more popular pet in the United States according to 2024 statistics from the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA). While this is likely not a casual relationship with media representation, there does seem to be some correlation.

That being said, United States shelter adoption statistics might surprise you! Below, you can explore data from Shelter Animal Count's 2024 report. Click the buttons below to switch between different views.
(Note that Average Adoptions = Total adoptions in the state / Total # of shelter organizations in the state.)

Even though dogs are the more popular pet, cats are more likely to be adopted from a shelter. We think the reason behind this is because there are much more dog breeds than cat breeds. Dogs are breeded for specific purposes (that's why there's so many more breeds of dogs!), so it's not unreasonable to think that when someone wants to adopt a dog, they'd rather get a specific breed from a breeder than go to a shelter. (Extra credit: Go ask a cat owner what breed their cat is. They'll likely say something like, "I don't know. Orange." Then ask a dog owner what breed their dog is. We bet that the dog owner will probably know what breed their dog is!)

Pet Products Overview

Data shows that almost twice the number of Dog toys were offered on the Market compared to Cat toys.


Additionally, Cat products as a whole tend to be more expensive than Dog products!


Evidently, Cat owners are way disadvantaged compared to Dog owners. But that does not have to be true.

When the Average Trade Amounts are compared, Cat owners actually beat the Dog owners by almost 1.5 times! Although, Cat owners are provided a smaller number of options, they prove to be more dedicated and passionate, buying the same products in higher quantities. Cat owners don't lack love nor care, just the lack of options! With a bit more media attention, and more marketing focus on cats, we can welcome a catty tomorrow, a feline future for all!
